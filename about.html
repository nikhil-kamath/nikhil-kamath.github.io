about this work

Poetry and food are not entirely synonymous, however, both mediums serve as an expression of the soul in a way. As long as humans have been alive, culinary practices have been a necessity. Similarly, as long as humans have been alive, a desire to express oneself has remained at the forefront of human nature. Therefore it’s not inconceivable to believe that food and poetry are more interconnected than one may think; food and poetry serve to enhance culture and highlight the uniqueness and strength of the human spirit. We have created a body of literature designed to educate the reader on great local restaurants and the literary style that supports it from a distance.

In short, we hope to accomplish a poetry anthology that celebrates local cuisines in their native poetic style. Poetry and food share a connection that transcends cultural boundaries. Just as food nourishes the body, poetry nourishes the soul, evoking emotions, memories, and identity. Various forms of poetry mirror their culinary background: Haiku, with its concise structure, reflects the simplicity and precision of Japanese cuisine. Lyrically complex Italian sonnets invoke a passionate emotion in the reader, just as the richness and depth of Italian dishes do.
