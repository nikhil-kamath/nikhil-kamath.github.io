Mihil's Letter to Halal lauds the relaxed atmosphere and calm ambience of the place. Despite not reinventing the wheel Halal Legends excels at what it offers. Fast, tasty and affordable food. The poem itself is written in Ghazal style. A style that originated in the Arabian peninsula as a form of Islamic poetry. The rhyme scheme consists of the form AA BA CA DA … where the last syllable of each line that ends in rhyme A usually is the same. Right before the A rhyme another rhyme called a radif precedes it thereby creating a two or more syllable rhyme at the end of each second line.

Mihil's Spice6 Diss Track was initially meant to celebrate traditional Indian poetry, he felt that the restaurant being reviewed was not true to their roots. He continued to say that describing the poem with Indian poetry would be disrespectful to Indian poetry. He instead decided to reflect the Americanized nature of the establishment by writing a diss track. The American diss track finds itself under the spotlight at the moment due to the ongoing feud between popular rappers, Drake and Kendrick Lamar. This diss track on Spice6 loosely follows the flow and cadence Kendrick Lamar employed on his menacing “Meet the Grahams”.

Mihil's Ode to Panang Curry is written in traditional Thai style - Khlong Si Suphap. The poetry style meant for a tonal language has been replicated in English. The poem structure itself is quite complicated with many rules to follow: Each stanza must have four lines composed of two hemistichs. The independent hemistichs, called “wak” in Thailand are laid out in two blocks. The poem is meant to be read line by line, jumping between hemistichs before progressing to the next line. Additionally the first hemistich must contain five syllables while the first three lines of the second hemistich contain 2 syllables and the fourth line’s second hemistich has four syllables. To appreciate the tonal language of Thai, the poem is read by alternating stressed and unstressed syllables.
